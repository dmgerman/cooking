5.1 Where does Vanilla come from?

Vanilla is native to Mexico, Guatemala and other parts of Central America. At the present time, it grows also in Madagascar, the Seychelles, Tahiti, Réunion and other tropical areas[#Mulherin##1#]. The first recorded use of the spice in European literature dates back to 1520, when Moctezuma II offered vanilla flavoured chocolate to Hernán Cortés. However, the use of <#81#> tlilxochitl<#81#> (Nahuatl for vanilla) is earlier documented in the precolumbian literature.
